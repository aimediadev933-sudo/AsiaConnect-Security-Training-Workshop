Project Overview

The SecureAsi@ Workshop strengthened cybersecurity capabilities of network engineers and administrators across AsiaConnect partner countries. Jointly organized by Universiti Sains Malaysia (USM), Malaysian Research and Education Network (MYREN), and University of Hamburg (UH), this intensive three-day training empowered participants to detect, manage, and mitigate cybersecurity threats in their National Research and Education Networks (NRENs).

🎯 Project Objectives

  • Enhance cybersecurity knowledge and practical skills of NREN engineers
  • Introduce foundational cybersecurity concepts and threats
  • Provide exposure to IDS, honeypots, sandboxes, and penetration testing
  • Train in incident detection and response
  • Encourage collaboration and best practices sharing across NRENs

Training Activities

Day 1: Security Foundations

  • Physical security principles
  • Malware analysis and detection
  • Botnet identification
  • DDoS attack mitigation
  • Social engineering awareness

Day 2: Detection & Defense

  • Linux/networking refresher
  • Intrusion Detection Systems (IDS)
  • Honeypot deployment
  • Sandbox analysis
  • Access control implementation

Day 3: Vulnerabilities & Hands-On

  • Vulnerability analysis techniques
  • Penetration testing methodologies
  • Capture-the-Flag (CTF) challenge
  • Practical security assessments
  • Certificate presentations

Key Outcomes

🔧 Improved Technical Skills

Participants learned to secure NREN infrastructure using advanced tools and techniques including IDS, honeypots, firewalls, and ethical hacking methodologies.

🤝 Enhanced Regional Collaboration

The workshop created a strong network among cybersecurity professionals across 13 AsiaConnect countries, fostering ongoing collaboration and knowledge sharing.

📚 Established Training Framework

Delivered a replicable cybersecurity training model combining theoretical knowledge, practical application, and peer learning opportunities.
